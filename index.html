Survival horror at its best

Resident Evil, Biohazard in Japan, is a survival horror game franchise owned by the famous game company Capcom.
Released on the Playstation in 1996, later to be given ports to the Sega Saturn and Pc with some added content.
The game was created and directed by Shinji Mikami, who later aslo directed the remade version released in 2002 on the Nintendo GameCube.
The franchise started out as a video game, later to expand to various movies, toys, novels, and what not.

The game takes place in a fictionary american town called Raccoon City, a city controlled by a large evil
pharmiceutical company named Umbrella. The story centers around S.T.A.R.S, a special unit team of the Raccoon City
Police Department consisting of two teams, the Alpha and the Bravo team. After outlandish reports of people being attacked and eaten in the
Arklay Mountains, S.T.A.R.S are dispatched to investigate, but the Bravo team then goes missing during the mission.

The Alpha team then starts searching and discovers the crashed Bravo team helicopter along with one of the members dead nearby.
The team then gets attacked upon their discovery by monstrous dogs resulting in the death of Joseph Frost, and utimately forcing the team to escape
into a large mansion where the story then unfolds.
